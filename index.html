Hi!

I'm Lynnsey.

I'm a native-born Austinite enduring the (painful) winters while I attend school at Northeastern University in Boston, MA to study computer science.

I began to code late in high school, where I was fortunate enough to find a program with enough hyper-intelligent individuals to bring out my competitive side. By the time I realized that I actually enjoyed coding, I was so invested in proving that I could do it that I couldn't have quit if I even wanted to.

As I started college, the experiences that I had (and loved) from high school eventually guided me to pursue CS for good. As I continue to study, explore, and gain experience in the field, I aim to combine many of the things I love into what I do every day. From big data to app development, I've found ways to dig deeper into all of the things I love.

Here, you'll be able to see some of the ways that I have begun to combine those ideas. Where software meets music or running or the environment or food (or perhaps all of those), that's where I hope to find my work and make an impact.

Cisco Software Engineering Coop

July 2018 - December 2018, San Jose CA

My first coop at Northeastern, I came to Cisco as a member of their NFVIS team, working on the operating system for enterprise devices like ENCS. During my time on the team, I worked on new client-facing features like Single IP and Secure Overlay.

Things I loved: amazing managers, interesting new challenges in every feature, open work atmosphere

Things I learned: work-life balance matters, test EVERYTHING, simple networking principles, basic knowledge of virtual machines and operating systems

Northeastern University Action Lab Research Assistant

September 2016 - May 2018, Boston MA

Coming into college, I hoped to get involved in research on campus immediately - and found a path to pursue that through a grant in the Northeastern Honors Program. I worked in the Action Lab as an Honors Undergraduate Early Research Assistant and myself getting involved in a number of different projects. From data classification to designing my first website, I began to appreciate the broader applications of computer science in other fields.

Things I loved: lots of independence and responsibility, the diversity of challenges, seeing a project from start to finish

Northeastern University SMILE Lab REU D3 Participant

June 2017 - August 2017, Boston MA

Applying for a research position for the summer after my sophomore year of college, I was accepted into a program through the National Science Foundation to study big data and machine learning. For 10 weeks, I worked in a machine learning research lab on Northeastern's campus to help refine algorithms that extracted temporally segmented features from videos in order to improve action prediction. At the end of the experience, I presented my research to a group of my peers, professors, and researchers.

Things I loved: learning the math behind machine learning, creating meaninful results in a lab setting

Things I learned: fundamentals of neural networks, statistics, how to write and read research papers, research is not my thing, broader knowledge of machine learning algorithms, the importance of communication in an office
